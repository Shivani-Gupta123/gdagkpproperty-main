Frequently Asked Questions

Real estate properties are generally categorized into residential, commercial, industrial, and land. Residential properties include apartments, houses, and villas. Commercial properties include office spaces, retail shops, and malls. Industrial properties include factories and warehouses. Land includes plots for construction, agricultural land, and undeveloped land.

The value of a property can be determined by several factors, including location, size, condition, and market trends. You can also get a professional appraisal or use online tools to estimate the value. Comparing similar properties in the same area can provide a good benchmark.

Before buying a property, consider factors such as your budget, the location, the condition of the property, future development plans in the area, and legal clearances. It's also important to inspect the property thoroughly and get a professional evaluation if necessary.

The steps involved in selling a property include setting a competitive price, preparing the property for sale, marketing it effectively, negotiating with potential buyers, and completing the necessary legal and financial paperwork. Hiring a real estate agent can help streamline the process.
